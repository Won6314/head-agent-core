# Discussion Questions

[HEAD Agent Core](../README.md) / [Teach](README.md) / Discussion Questions

Use these prompts with public-safe, fictional, or participant-owned examples. Do not solicit credentials, private paths, production details, or copied instruction bodies.

## Architecture And Engineering

1. Where could an unchecked generated assumption become an interface, test, or acceptance condition downstream?
2. What direct evidence would be stronger than a worker's confidence statement for that claim?
3. Which part of a proposed solution is a bounded outcome rather than a step list?
4. What would make a component boundary unclear in your system: Core, project context, runtime canon, Skill, MCP, or Agent?

Related: [One-Step Expansion](../learn/02-llm-problem/the-one-step-expansion-rule.md), [Verification Before Expansion](../learn/02-llm-problem/verification-before-expansion.md), [Components](../learn/07-components/README.md).

## Product And Design

1. Which choices change the meaning of success enough that the User must make them?
2. What may HEAD plan without reopening material direction?
3. How would a broad context dump change the quality of a design or product decision?
4. What is the smallest observable result a specialist could own without inventing policy?

Related: [Decision Rights](../learn/03-ownership/decision-rights.md), [Context By Ownership](../learn/04-context/context-by-ownership.md).

## AI Operations

1. Which facts must remain canonical if work stops after a local result but before integration?
2. When is a summary useful, and why must it not redefine the user-HEAD agreement?
3. What evidence should HEAD inspect before allowing a result to become the premise for another task?
4. When would independent challenge add useful perspective rather than unnecessary ceremony?

Related: [Fixing The Problem And Goal](../learn/06-canon/fixing-the-problem-and-goal.md), [Verification Before Expansion](../learn/02-llm-problem/verification-before-expansion.md#three-verification-levels).

## Workshop Exercises

1. Invent a small public scenario. Write one user-fixed goal, one material choice, one bounded outcome, and its completion evidence.
2. Sort the scenario's information into project-owned canon, HEAD working context, and Agent assignment context.
3. Identify a generated claim that requires verification before another team or tool relies on it.
4. Compare a one-shot worker with an autonomous swarm for this scenario. What problem does each create or avoid?
5. Choose a minimal adoption level and state what you deliberately will not add yet.

Related: [Workshop route](120-minute-workshop.md), [Why Not An Autonomous Swarm](../learn/09-decisions/why-not-an-autonomous-swarm.md), [Minimal Adoption](../learn/11-adoption/minimal-adoption.md).

## Whole-Group Close

1. What is one decision that must remain visibly owned by the User?
2. What is one result HEAD must integrate rather than merely accept?
3. What is one evidence gate you will add before further expansion?
4. What is one durable fact you would preserve for recovery?

Close by restating the course thesis: controlled expansion depends on explicit ownership, relevant authority, durable direction, and verification before the next expansion.
