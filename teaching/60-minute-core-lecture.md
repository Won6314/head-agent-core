# 60-Minute Core Lecture

[HEAD Agent Core](../README.md) / [Teach](README.md) / 60-Minute Core Lecture

## Outcome

Participants can connect the system's origin, LLM problem model, ownership model, context authority, durable canon, and operating loop. They can distinguish Historical record and Operational observation from Related theory used as a later interpretation.

## Preparation

- Open [the controlled-expansion loop](diagrams.md#controlled-expansion-loop), [context by ownership](diagrams.md#context-by-ownership), and [the durable work agreement](diagrams.md#durable-work-agreement).
- Use the [Library Kiosk Pilot](../learn/08-operation/end-to-end-example.md) only as a synthetic example.

## Teaching Notes

When describing the origin, retain the course's evidence labels. Historical record and Operational observation describe what was supported or observed; Related theory is a retrospective explanatory lens, not proof of original intent.

Do not turn the context section into a request to preload more material. The point is that project canon, HEAD's work model, and a worker's bounded assignment have different owners and different necessary breadth.

## Suggested Slide Sequence

1. Course thesis and the controlled-expansion loop.
2. A short Historical record transition from one session to specialized roles.
3. Decision rights and the context-by-ownership diagram.
4. The durable work agreement and the component loop.
5. One reflection prompt and the minimal next action.
